# Fix clearance zone rendering: overlapping areas & square pad shapes

## What changed
- **Removed evenodd clip bug**: replaced `ctx.clip('evenodd')` + `fillRect` with an offscreen
  canvas per zone. When two clearance cutouts overlapped, the evenodd rule would flip the
  intersection back to "filled" (phantom copper). With the new `destination-out` compositing
  approach, overlapping clearances simply erase — they can never re-create copper.
- **Rectangular clearance for square/rect pads**: previously all pads used a circular arc for
  their clearance cutout. Now `rect`/`square` pads get a rotated rectangular clearance that
  exactly follows the pad outline (corners computed analytically — no ctx.save/rotate needed).
- **No-net copper gets clearance too**: the old guard `if(!p.net || p.net===z.net) continue`
  skipped pads with no net entirely. Changed to `if(p.net && p.net===z.net) continue` so
  copper without a net still gets the correct clearance gap in the zone fill.
- Same fix applied consistently to vias and traces.

## Files
- `frontend/static/js/pcb-editor.js` — `_drawZones()` method fully rewritten

## Notes
- The offscreen canvas is sized to the zone's screen bounding box (+ margin) for performance,
  not the full canvas size.
- Trace capsule shape is unchanged; the end-cap arcs remain the same CW direction since they
  are now drawn into an `destination-out` context where winding direction doesn't affect fill.
