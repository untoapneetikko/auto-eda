# Wire free-end anchoring when dragging a component

## What changed
- When you drag a component, the wire endpoints attached to its pins already rubber-banded (moved with the pin). Now the **other end** of each stretched wire is explicitly anchored:
  - At drag-start the original position of every "free" endpoint is recorded (`otherPtOrig`).
  - During drag the free endpoint is locked back to that recorded position every frame — it can no longer drift, even if the same wire is referenced multiple times in the connection list.
  - On mouse-up the free endpoint is snapped to the nearest component port or net-label within tolerance (30 px), so it re-pins precisely rather than floating near the pin.
- A **green ring + dot** now appears at each pinned endpoint while dragging, giving clear visual feedback that those endpoints are "held" to their pins.
- Same logic applied to group drags (`_buildGroupDragItems`).

## Notes
- Wires whose **both** ends touch ports of the same dragged component are correctly identified and excluded from anchoring — they translate as a unit, which is the right behaviour for intra-component wires.
